The Problem

Real Madrid asked for a visualization tool. The actual problem is bigger.

They have years of data across different digital products, but the real issue is not “we need charts.” The real issue is that nobody has one recurring system that tells leadership what changed, what matters, and what needs attention first.

What a normal project would do
  • Build a dashboard with filters and line charts
  • Show monthly trends for each digital asset
  • Add a few AI summaries
  • Stop at “here are some insights”
What we want to do instead
  • Build a product that updates every month
  • Rank the most important issues and opportunities
  • Show RM against peers where benchmark data is real
  • Turn scattered metrics into a repeatable business workflow
Useful sentence here: “The value is not that we found one cool insight once. The value is that the same product keeps delivering useful answers every month.”
The Data

What we actually have, in plain English

This is monthly data, not event-stream data. That matters. It means we should build a strong monthly operating product, not fake day-level precision.

Internal Real Madrid data

Four digital areas with rich monthly metrics: main website, eCommerce, streaming, and fan app.

  • Website traffic and engagement
  • eCommerce funnel and sales metrics
  • Streaming usage and subscriptions
  • App usage, downloads, and loyalty signals

Benchmark data

Five peer clubs on selected comparable metrics. Not everything is benchmarked, so we must stay honest and narrow.

  • Traffic and engagement comparisons
  • eCommerce conversion and cart value
  • Streaming engagement signals
  • App usage and recurrence

What this means

We can compare Real Madrid against competitors on selected KPIs and we can analyze how RM’s own four digital businesses move together over time.

Explain the limitation clearly: “We do not have every metric for every club, so the product must benchmark only the KPIs that the benchmark file actually supports.”
The Product

ClubOS is a digital business operating system for football clubs

The product is designed to behave like internal SaaS: same app, same workflow, same questions answered every month when the next files arrive.

The core promise

New data comes in. ClubOS recalculates the latest health, peer context, signal relationships, and ranked priorities. The business team gets a clear view of what deserves attention now.

Say it this way: “The hero is not the chart. The hero is the ranked list of what the business team should care about this month.”

The product sentence

ClubOS unifies website, eCommerce, streaming, and app data, compares selected KPIs to peer clubs, detects business-relevant signals, and ranks the issues and opportunities leadership should investigate next.

How It Works

The monthly operating loop

This is the workflow that makes the product feel like SaaS instead of a one-time academic analysis.

01

New monthly files arrive

The same kind of internal and benchmark files get uploaded again in the next reporting cycle.

02

Databricks pipeline cleans and standardizes them

Raw files are preserved, names are normalized, metrics are validated, and app-safe tables are generated.

03

ClubOS recalculates the logic

KPI health, benchmark gaps, leading signals, and monthly priorities update automatically.

04

The business team opens the same product

They see what changed, what matters, and what needs investigation. Same workflow. New answers.

Core Modules

What the product actually contains

These are not random screens. Each module answers one business question and supports the same monthly operating loop.

1. Priority Board

Hero module

The ranked list of the most important issues and opportunities this month. This is the first screen and the most important part of the product.

2. Command Center

MVP

A clean view of how the four digital businesses are performing right now against baseline expectations.

3. Peer Benchmark Engine

MVP

Real Madrid versus peer clubs on the metrics that the benchmark dataset genuinely supports.

4. Commercial Signal Engine

MVP

A small, disciplined set of 1–3 month leading relationships that help explain what may matter next, especially around commercial outcomes.

5. Event Intelligence

Later, not hero

A curated event layer that helps explain major historical shifts. Useful, but not the center of the product.

If the team asks what makes this feel different, answer: “The product is organized around a recurring business workflow, not around a list of charts.”

Buzzword Translator

Plain-English meanings for the terms we will use

This is here so nobody in the team gets lost in product, data, or architecture language.

SaaS product Software people keep using repeatedly, not just a one-time analysis. In our case: new monthly data comes in, same product keeps producing updated outputs.
Databricks pipeline The backend data process that takes messy files, cleans them, validates them, and turns them into reliable tables for the app.
Bronze / Silver / Gold Three data stages: raw files, cleaned data, and app-ready business tables.
Priority Board The ranked list of the most important things the business team should look at right now.
Benchmark Comparing Real Madrid to peer clubs, but only on the metrics the benchmark data truly supports.
Leading signal A metric that tends to move before another metric, for example one that tends to come before stronger eCommerce results.
Gold table A final trusted dataset the product can safely read from without doing heavy logic in the UI.
Recurring monthly refresh The same workflow repeated when next month’s files are uploaded. This is what makes the product reusable.
How To Present It

A short talk track you can use in the call

If you want to explain the whole idea in a calm, clear way, this sequence works well.

1. Start with the problem “The task looked like a visualization project at first, but the real opportunity is bigger. The data can support a recurring internal product, not just a one-time dashboard.”
2. Explain the data honestly “We have monthly internal data across four digital products and selected benchmark data for peer clubs. So the best product is a monthly operating system, not a fake real-time engine.”
3. Say what ClubOS is “ClubOS is a digital business operating system that updates every month and tells the team what changed, what matters, and where to focus next.”
4. Emphasize the hero feature “The most important screen is the Priority Board. That is what makes it feel like a product instead of a chart collection.”
5. Explain why it can live beyond the assignment “Because the same type of files will keep coming in the future, the same product can keep running and delivering value after this first snapshot.”
